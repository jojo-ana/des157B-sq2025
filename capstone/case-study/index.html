Project Overview

"The American Dream" is an interactive narrative project that explores how the pursuit of this ideology that is now rooted in individual success and social mobility has gradually eroded the sense of community and collective wellbeing that originally founded it.

Targeted to individuals from working-class or modest economic backgrounds, this highlights the expectations of the dream and the reality of it.

Through visual storytelling and mini-interactions via game and animations, this critically examines the historical evolution of the American Dream, ultimately advocating for re-centering community and economic fairness as essential to a sustainable and equitable future

Project Research

Historical / Contemporary Sources

I drew from sources analyzing the American Dream’s shift from a collective goal of equality to an individualistic pursuit tied to inequality and economic challenges. This research highlights its complexity and how historical, economic, and social factors have shaped its evolving meaning.

Key themes: rising inequality, the shift from collective well-being to individual success, and the American Dream’s role as a rhetorical tool that both inspires and masks social challenges, emphasizing the need to address economic and social disparities in the narrative.

Informed by these insights, the design aimed to balance critical perspectives with accessibility, using clear storytelling to engage users emotionally and intellectually.

Comparative Analysis

The intent for both Art Militonian's website on the War on Ukraine and The Search for my kimchi by Alvin Chang is to highlight two different design approaches to storytelling. One informs and inspires through data-driven storytelling with dynamic visuals/interactions. The other focuses on creating an accessible, game-like experience that encourages exploration and learning at the user’s own pace.

Usability Testing

Additionally, I conducted periodic usability studies throughout the process to ensure user flow made sense

  • Information Timing: Participants prefer showing information during the puzzle to naturally break up content and boost engagement and retention.
  • Visual Effects: Visuals improve the experience; increase interactive elements like subtle sounds and completion feedback.
  • Future Section Aesthetic: Design the future section with a tech-focused, futuristic style featuring robotics, transparency, and space-age themes inspired by late-stage capitalism or “Jetsons.”
  • Page Structure: The structure effectively balances content and gameplay. Add clearer interaction cues and shorten pop-up text for easier reading.

Visual Design

Page Structure

The page structure is consistent across all three scenes, providing a unified framework for content and interaction.

To enhance the visual experience, I integrated animation and interaction libraries such as GSAP, Animate on Scroll, jQuery UI, Particles.js, and Three.js, which added dynamic and engaging effects throughout the site.

At the same time, I made each section visually distinct to evoke a sense of traveling through different time periods.

Scene 1

For Scene 1, I focused on creating a sense of the past while setting clear expectations for the puzzle interactions through a simple and intuitive design.

The puzzle in Scene 1 takes the form of a ripped newspaper to reflect how the past is often reconstructed from fragmented accounts and historical records. Newspapers symbolize archived truth, and the torn pieces represent how history is incomplete, subjective, and pieced together over time. This visual metaphor sets the tone for the user to engage in reconstructing the past

Scene 2

For Scene 2, I leaned into a modern aesthetic by using Corporate Memphis-style visuals. This modern, corporate-friendly visual language mirrors how success is now marketed—through tech companies, social media platforms, and productivity tools that emphasize individual achievement, brand identity, and economic performance.

Version 1

Initially, I had drawn out the puzzle pieces, but it had not visually represented the time line, so I took it and modernized it

Version 2

It then looked like puzzle pieces on a phone, but it was not modern enough

Version 3

To make the order easier to notice but harder to solve, I hid the clues inside the images themselves. This makes players look more carefully and think a bit harder to figure out the right sequence, encouraging active engagement.

Scene 3

For Scene 3, I explored what a “future” aesthetic might look like by leaning into 3D visuals. 3D is often used in futuristic design trends, so incorporating it helped reinforce the idea of moving forward in time while also making the experience feel more immersive and tech-driven.

User Interface

Puzzle

Each scene features a drag-and-drop puzzle that reinforces the theme visually and interactively. Pieces are placed on the sides and must be arranged into a central grid, encouraging exploration and engagement. Using jQuery UI kept interactions smooth while allowing for a consistent yet flexible structure across all three time periods.

Hover

Hover gave more insight, whether it be informationally or just in hints for the puzzles.

Revisions

Visual Iteration

With each iteration of the website, I leaned heavier into the visual design of the website.

Hover and Chunking

By breaking content into manageable, interactive pieces, it keeps users engaged and encourages deeper understanding without cognitive overload.

Error Checker

Implemented a clear visual indicator to highlight incorrect puzzle pieces, providing immediate feedback and helping users correct mistakes efficiently.

Summary

"The American Dream" is an interactive web experience that examines how the dream has shifted from collective wellbeing to individual success. Targeted at working-class users, the site uses storytelling, drag-and-drop puzzles, and time-based visuals to explore past, present, and future ideologies.

Each scene incorporates distinct visual themes—from a torn newspaper reflecting historical fragmentation, to Corporate Memphis critiques of modern ambition, to 3D environments imagining future possibilities. Built with GSAP, jQuery UI, Three.js and others, the project prioritizes having a strong visual identity and interactive storytelling.
